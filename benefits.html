Advantages of Plastic


Affordable and Lightweight: Plastic is a more affordable and lighter alternative to many natural materials.

Versatile and Moldable: The name "PLASTIC" comes from the Greek word "plastikos" meaning "capable of being molded or shaped."
This unique property allows it to be formed into various shapes, making it useful for countless applications.

Strong and Durable: During World War II, plastic was a durable, heat-resistant, and electrical-insulating substitute for scarce metals, proving to be a cheap, lightweight, and strong material.

Durable: It was used in essential items like telephones, radios, and electrical appliances.


Disadvantages of Plastic


Environmental Pollution: Plastic waste has become a global crisis. It harms marine life, pollutes soil and rivers, and clogs drinking water sources.

Harm to Human Health: Plastics release harmful chemicals, such as BPA and phthalates, which can enter our food and water.
Diseases linked with plastic exposure include cancer, respiratory issues, hormonal imbalance, and digestive problems.

Non-Biodegradable: Unlike natural materials, plastic does not break down easily and can remain in the environment for centuries.
